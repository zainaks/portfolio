0-to-1 Project

ParkSwift

A team project to build an app for renting and selling parking spaces in the city Ann Arbor, Michigan.

Team

VOID Tech organization at the University of Michigan

Status

In Development

Timeline

October 2023 - Current (1 Year)

Role

Independently responsible for the construction and design of facets and filtration system.

Problem Statement

Users had to brute search through postings to find the offers they desire.

Before the Prototypes

The design process began with initial conceptualization, focusing on how filtering functionality could be integrated. Initially envisioned as a supplementary add-on feature, rather than a standalone page, it quickly became evident through a brief assessment of possible user needs that a dedicated filtering page was necessary.

Design Process

  • Information Architecture

  • Iteration #1

    After identifying user requirements, the focus shifted to balancing comprehensive functionality with a clean, concise layout. The goal was to ensure all filter options were visible and accessible to users, without overwhelming them with visual clutter or excessive interactions.

    A significant portion of this iteration involved determining the optimal input methods for users to specify preferences, such as selecting "Up to $10 per hour" versus "Less than $300 per month." This process required careful consideration of usability principles and the design of input systems that were both flexible and user-friendly.

  • Iteration #2

    The first iteration revealed several usability challenges, prompting significant refinements. Mobile apps like Uber were referenced to evaluate effective interaction patterns and structural layouts that align with user expectations.

    In this iteration, special attention was given to ergonomic design principles, particularly the "Thumb Zone Rule," to enhance comfort and accessibility for mobile users. A key improvement involved replacing the overly small navigation bar with dropdown menus, which provided a more practical and user-friendly solution for accessing filter options.

    Additionally, collaboration with team members facilitated the integration of filtering features, such as parking-related tags, directly onto posts. This enhancement improved the overall user experience by streamlining navigation and enabling more intuitive filtering functionality.

Results

The initial challenge stemmed from the app's lack of a proper faceted search system, which significantly hindered usability and user satisfaction. By identifying key facets, designing an intuitive structure to support them, and creating a user-friendly navigation system, I developed a well-organized and effective faceted search system prototype.

The newly designed filtering system, combined with the implementation of parking-related tags, substantially enhanced the app’s functionality and usability. These improvements are expected to result in significantly reduced user task completion times and increased flexibility in meeting user needs. With these refinements, the app is now better positioned to receive positive feedback in a System Usability Scale (SUS) assessment.
