Opportunity

In my sophomore year of college, I was accepted into VOID Technology, an organization at my university that brings together student designers, developers, and product managers to produce one to two annual online products. After all of our collective ideas for the year were taken into consideration, one of the products chosen was ParkSwift, a mobile app which would focus on buying and renting parking spaces in Ann Arbor, Michigan.

Starting Off As a Team

  • Crazy 8s Design Sprint

    This was a sprint activity done together as a team, meant to garner more ideas for the initial low fidelity prototype.

  • Referencing, Finding Needs

    We searched for different mobile sites with features that we felt were proper for the app’s purpose.

  • Building The Design System

    After the low fidelity prototype was completed, a simple design system, including the typography and color scheme, was implemented onto the prototype.

My Main Contribution

  • Front Page Navigation

    The low fidelity prototype began with the front page, which called for a scrolling system wherein users could quickly understand what is being rented on this app, and the features these rentals provide to them.
    I created the initial design for the renting boxes, and collaborated with other designers afterwards to improve structure and symmetry. There were several different possible designs I could implement, however I chose the one that would be best eligible for all users.

  • Filtering System

    I assigned myself to focus on the filtering pages while the rest of the team chose their own roles. The filtering system was an unexpected learning curve for me to implement—It was my first time designing a mobile app, and I was not well situated with their filtering structures. After referencing multiple different apps, I was able to create a page that could cover all the needed filtering options, while also reducing the amount of cluster and overwhelmness for users.
